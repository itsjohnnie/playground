# Zach Lieberman — Deep Research Document for a Daily Generative-Art Practice

Compiled 2026-07-06. Sources inline. Everything technical here is aimed at reproducing effects in WebGL/canvas.

---

## 1. Who He Is

**Zachary Lieberman** (b. 1977, USA; based in New York) — new media artist, programmer, and educator. His self-description: he makes artwork with code, focused on "human gesture as input, amplified in different ways — making drawings come to life, imagining what the voice might look like if we could see it."

- **Education:** BA Fine Arts, Hunter College (painting/printmaking); BFA + MFA Design & Technology, Parsons (MFA 2002). His pivot to code came via **Macromedia Flash** — timelines plus scripting revealed software's expressive potential. He has said the generosity of open-source communities reminded him of collaborative printmaking studios, which directly motivated openFrameworks ([Layer interview](https://layer.com/artists/zach-lieberman/interview), [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zachary_Lieberman)).
- **openFrameworks** — co-created with Theo Watson and Arturo Castro: the open-source C++ toolkit for creative coding that anchored a generation of installation artists. Nearly all his daily sketches are written in it (Xcode, C++, GLSL).
- **School for Poetic Computation (SFPC)** — co-founded 2013 in NYC; "a hybrid of a school, residency and research group" examining "the lyrical possibilities of code" ([sfpc.io](https://sfpc.io/people/zach-lieberman/)).
- **MIT Media Lab** — joined 2019 as adjunct associate professor, directs the **Future Sketches** group ([MIT News](https://news.mit.edu/2019/zach-lieberman-joins-mit-media-lab-1125), [Media Lab page](https://www.media.mit.edu/people/zachl/overview/)). He teaches art history alongside code — Vera Molnár, John Whitney, Muriel Cooper are staples of his syllabus ([Le Random](https://www.lerandom.art/editorial/zach-lieberman)).
- **Key earlier works:** *Messa di Voce* and *Manual Input Sessions* with Golan Levin (voice/hand gesture → live audiovisuals); **EyeWriter** (2009, with Tempt1, Evan Roth, James Powderly et al.) — open-source eye-tracking drawing device for the paralyzed graffiti writer Tony Quan; won the **Ars Electronica Golden Nica** (2010), exhibited at MoMA, listed in Time's Best Inventions. Also *Drawn* (interactive painting performance), YesYesNo studio installations, Fast Company "100 Most Creative People" (#36, 2010) ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zachary_Lieberman)).
- Recent institutional presence: *Atlas of Blobs* at M+ Hong Kong ([mplus.org.hk](https://www.mplus.org.hk/en/exhibitions/zachary-lieberman-atlas-of-blobs/)), *Circles, Blobs, Ripples* at Unit London ([unitlondon.com](https://unitlondon.com/exhibitions/zachary-lieberman-circles-blobs-ripples/)), *10 Years of Daily Sketches* at Nguyen Wahed London, Jan 2026 ([nguyenwahed.com](https://nguyenwahed.com/exhibitions/33-10-years-of-daily-sketches-zach-lieberman-london/overview/)), *Sketches* exhibit at the Exploratorium ([exploratorium.edu](https://www.exploratorium.edu/exhibits/sketches)).

## 2. The Daily Sketch Practice

**Facts:** Started January 1, 2016 as a New Year's resolution, posted to Instagram [@zach.lieberman](https://www.instagram.com/zach.lieberman/). Inspired by Reza Ali's daily sketches. Ongoing 10+ years; by early 2026 he counted **~5,722 posts (~1.56/day)**. Deep personal origin: he began after his father's death in 2015, wanting a less pressurized creative life after years of secretive months-long projects that could disappoint on release ([10-year retrospective](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/i-spent-10-years-making-a-sketch-in-code-every-day-and-heres-what-i-learned-b845e811160d)). The sweetest origin detail: he coded sketches in his step-daughter's room while she fell asleep and showed her the results in the morning; her feedback ("that's hypnotizing me!", "you should try color") shaped early work ([2016 post](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/daily-sketches-2016-28586d8f008e)).

**Time budget:** ~30 minutes per sketch (up to an hour). "I sketch up until the point I think it's interesting, record it, post it and clock out." He deliberately works "as messy and mindlessly as possible — I don't plan, I just see where the wind blows." Sketching is "the opposite of work."

**Pipeline (2016–present, barely changed):** code in openFrameworks/Xcode → QuickTime screen record (often 640×640) → ffmpeg compress (`ffmpeg -i INPUT.mov -crf 10 -vf "scale=640:640, setpts=1.0*PTS" -c:a copy -tune grain OUTPUT.mov`) → AirDrop to iPhone → post. Everything lives in one giant chronological "everyday" folder (100+ GB). He uses **SrcSnap** (Lingdong Huang) to auto-commit code to git every time he screenshots.

**His rules (2016):** avoid overused algorithms (Delaunay, reaction-diffusion); minimize randomness in favor of `sin()`/`tan()`; reinterpret inspiration; take no requests ("DJ does not take requests!"); don't fear repetition; post the moment something crosses the threshold; surprise yourself. In 2017 he added a ban on Delaunay, particle systems, and HSB color, plus the motto **"ABI: Always Be Iterating."**

**Philosophy quotes (all sourceable):**
- "Creativity is more about tedium, doing the same thing again and again, almost like a meditation, and that what matters are the shortcuts, the breaks, the changes, the alterations you make." ([2017](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/daily-sketches-in-2017-1b4234b0615d))
- "These sketches are like diary entries. When I think back to a sketch, I sometimes remember the specific day, where I was, what the temperature was like." ([2016](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/daily-sketches-2016-28586d8f008e))
- "Make something new out of something old. Every day I'm often taking code from the day before, and remixing it to see what it tells me." ([Nguyen Wahed](https://nguyenwahed.com/exhibitions/33-10-years-of-daily-sketches-zach-lieberman-london/overview/))
- "My favorite discoveries are completely accidental. They happen by just pushing and pushing and pushing until things break, but sometimes they break in beautiful ways." ([2023](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/daily-sketching-in-2023-b4c0e31c8acb))
- "I'm not always sure I'm making anything new, but I'm new, my eyes are new." ([2025](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/daily-sketching-in-2025-60189ffd6b60))
- "I often oscillate between intense noise/chaos and simplicity." ([2021](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/daily-sketching-in-2021-fc92220aa3f4))
- "I'm always happy with computer graphics that don't feel exactly like computer graphics but are organic, sketchy and a bit wild." ([2018](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/daily-sketching-in-2018-f5de6d284aac))
- On his role: create "a kind of grammar, and then experiment with all the different forms that grammar can create." ([2023](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/daily-sketching-in-2023-b4c0e31c8acb))
- "Color to me is about feeling." Red should be intense, "like blood or something." ([Layer](https://layer.com/artists/zach-lieberman/interview))
- The code itself "is usually really messy and very idiosyncratic. It's like my sketchbook." He rejects process-fetishism — like asking painters about canvas stretching ([Le Random](https://www.lerandom.art/editorial/zach-lieberman)).

**The 10 lessons from the 10-year post** ([full post](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/i-spent-10-years-making-a-sketch-in-code-every-day-and-heres-what-i-learned-b845e811160d)): 1) Always be iterating — repetition creates "desire paths" that become your style. 2) Document everything in one folder. 3) Reduce friction to publish. 4) You're doing it for yourself (quotes rapper JME: "Music is reason I make music"). 5) See through others' eyes — comments reveal which ideas resonate. 6) Repeating yourself is valuable. 7) Speed matters; imperfection is acceptable (echoes Beeple: pick something doable in 5 minutes, "since there will be days when you only have five minutes"). 8) **Boredom drives innovation** — sit with dissatisfaction until breakthroughs emerge. 9) Silence external voices. 10) The goal is **freedom** — to fail, repeat, get lost, create for its own sake. Sign-off motto: **K.G. — Keep Going.**

## 3. The Visual Language — Technical Catalog

His palette tendencies across all of this: **high-chroma gradients glowing on dark/black grounds** (light-emissive, "illuminated" look, additive mixing), and a second register of **soft pastel/watercolor fields** ("1960s illustration… almost watercolor and a bit psychedelic"). He avoids HSB rainbow-cycling; color comes from light-mixing math and hand-tuned palettes. Motion is almost always **perfectly looping** (phase = `TWO_PI * frameNum / loopFrames`), sine-driven rather than random.

### 3.1 Blobs (the signature)
- **Soft-body spring blobs:** particles connected by springs in a closed loop; inter-particle repulsion force ∝ 1/distance with a max interaction radius; internal pressure keeps it inflated. Result: lifelike wobble with no keyframing. Later: blob-blob collision via springs; "blob packs"; "blob inside blob inside blob"; 2022 refinement using **dot products of line segments to determine repulsion**, producing expressive "arms." 2025: rebuilt with **Box2D joint constraints** for cleaner "line blob" forms ([2016](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/daily-sketches-2016-28586d8f008e), [2021](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/daily-sketching-in-2021-fc92220aa3f4), [2022](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/daily-sketching-in-2022-4160eac26aa8), [2025](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/daily-sketching-in-2025-60189ffd6b60)).
- **Metaballs:** implicit field `Σ rᵢ²/((x−xᵢ)²+(y−yᵢ)²)`, contoured with **marching squares/cubes**; his twist is slicing the field at **multiple thresholds** to get nested, layered organic contours (topographic-map blobs). Alternative: **stacked 2D slices of 3D simplex noise** rendered as layered contours, with amplitude modulation (noise × noise) ([2017](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/daily-sketches-in-2017-1b4234b0615d)).
- **Pseudo-3D shading trick:** triangulate the blob interior and color vertices by **distance to nearest edge + angle of that edge** — fake shading that makes flat 2D shapes read as inflated 3D. Also true inflation via the **Teddy algorithm** (openTeddy). This "2D that reads as 3D" ambiguity is a core aesthetic goal he states explicitly.
- Related helpers: ofxClipper (boolean/negative-space), ofxScribbleSegmenter (self-intersection-free polygon offsetting), CGAL straight skeletons.

### 3.2 Color / gradient studies ("color spots", "cone gradients")
His most imitated body of work. Premise (from [Verse](https://verse.works/series/color-gradient-study-by-zach-lieberman)): **every pixel's color is derived from a small set of moving color points, each with hue, intensity, and direction; one simple equation mixes them like light sources** — i.e., per-pixel additive/weighted blending in a fragment shader, "essentially photographs of math." WebGL recipe: pass N points (pos, RGB, direction, intensity) as uniforms; per fragment accumulate `color += pointColor * f(distance, angleBetween(fragDir, pointDir))`; falloff choice (1/d, exp(−d²), smoothstep) totally changes the mood. He "iterates the system to explore different feelings," gravitating to results that feel **illuminated**. Sine/tan applied to distance values creates focus/banding effects. Roots: Book of Shaders (Patricio Gonzalez Vivo & Jen Lowe) distance-field shading ([2016](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/daily-sketches-2016-28586d8f008e), [2019](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/daily-sketching-in-2019-c1c801045577)).
- **Poisson fill** (Lingdong Huang's oF implementation) for seamless soft color blending — solve Laplace's equation from boundary colors; in WebGL do it as an iterative convolution pyramid ([2020](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/daily-sketching-in-2020-c2b4a59057e5)).
- **Feedback displacement:** render-to-texture ping-pong where each frame the texture lookup is offset by a noise function — colors smear and spread organically ([2019](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/daily-sketching-in-2019-c1c801045577)).
- **Atmosphere shaders:** GLSL single-scattering sunset/sky algorithms with the physical constants (planet radius, sun position) deliberately mis-set — "playing with these leads to quite interesting results" ([2020](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/daily-sketching-in-2020-c2b4a59057e5)).

### 3.3 SDFs and distance fields (constant tool)
"I'm a big fan of distance fields… I work with distance images and SDF functions in shaders constantly." For arbitrary shapes (typography, body silhouettes) he **computes a distance image on the CPU/OpenCV or via jump flooding and passes it as a texture** to the shader, then drives gradients, glows, contour lines, and distortions off it. 2023 light studies were built on 2D circle SDFs + directional lighting + stacked/nested elements combined pairwise (a+b, a+c…) ([2020](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/daily-sketching-in-2020-c2b4a59057e5), [2023](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/daily-sketching-in-2023-b4c0e31c8acb)).

### 3.4 Light, neon, glow (2022–2025 arc)
2022 was "a year of light research" (inspired by his partner Molmol Kuo's neon practice): **neon glow via distance fields computed with the Jump Flood Algorithm** (JFA — O(log n) passes, perfect for WebGL) then exponential falloff glow; grids of lights treated "almost as giant pixels, like when you zoom into an LCD screen." **Iridescence** fascination (oil slicks on wet streets) → thin-film-interference-style palettes, i.e., `0.5+0.5*cos(TWO_PI*(freq*t+phaseRGB))` cosine palettes per channel. 2023: making the light algorithm "more painterly," chromatic aberration (per-channel offset sampling), brushed-metal anisotropic streaks. 2024: **water caustics** on the East River, box-intersection light studies, "light almost breaks the composition." 2025: return to light, with Tadao Ando / James Turrell as references — "softness and airiness" ([2022](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/daily-sketching-in-2022-4160eac26aa8), [2023](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/daily-sketching-in-2023-b4c0e31c8acb), [2024](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/daily-sketching-in-2024-77713b80f25d), [2025](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/daily-sketching-in-2025-60189ffd6b60)).

### 3.5 Reflection / refraction / lenses
- 2016 "light studies": 2D ray tracing — rays bounced off polygonal walls (accelerated with a BVH), including spelling text in Hershey fonts purely via reflected rays; later colored refraction.
- 2022–2024 "**virtual lenses**": magnifying-glass elements that pass over color fields, refracting/distorting them — in shader terms, a UV displacement inside a lens SDF (offset ∝ lens normal × strength), often producing painterly smears; "glassy textures and organic forms" ([2022](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/daily-sketching-in-2022-4160eac26aa8), [2024](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/daily-sketching-in-2024-77713b80f25d)).

### 3.6 Bands, stripes, moiré, venetian blinds
- "**Band studies**" (2021): grids where GLSL `floor()` and `fract()` chop noise fields into venetian-blind slats — childhood memory of light through blinds. Became the *Color Blind Studies* installation and IBM HQ artwork.
- **Moiré/interference** (2024): overlapping near-identical patterns (concentric rings, line gratings) at slightly different frequencies/centers so beat patterns emerge; he uses it for "chaotic visual energy" that "feels alive" ([2021](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/daily-sketching-in-2021-fc92220aa3f4), [2024](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/daily-sketching-in-2024-77713b80f25d)).

### 3.7 Typography deformation
Recurring: fonts as raw geometry. Slice-point detection so letters distort without breaking; text wrapped onto body contours (Margaret Atwood *Hag-Seed* performance); SDFs of glyphs driving gradients; **depth-of-field type** by pre-rendering ~50 blur levels of a letter and cross-fading (cheap bokeh); letters as spring-hinge particle chains ("letter morphing via angular forces"); "using every font" as a way to study designers' decisions; 2025 physarum-grown typography ([2016](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/daily-sketches-2016-28586d8f008e), [2018](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/daily-sketching-in-2018-f5de6d284aac), [2021](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/daily-sketching-in-2021-fc92220aa3f4)).

### 3.8 Faces and bodies
- **Más Que la Cara** (Houston, YesYesNo): street-facing interactive mask installation. Stack: dlib HoG face detection + CLM tracking of **68 landmarks** in openFrameworks; **CLAHE** preprocessing for bad lighting; visuals in **Paper.js in Chrome**, face data sent via **OSC over WebSockets**; aesthetic brief was "a poster based on your face," not a novelty filter — informed by Bruno Munari's *Design as Art*, pareidolia, and community cardboard-mask workshops ([project writeup](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/m%C3%A1s-que-la-cara-overview-48331a0202c0)). Directly portable to web via MediaPipe FaceMesh.
- Face sketches: quadtree portraits (recursive subdivision where color variance is high → detail concentrates at eyes), segmentation-based region growth, brightness-sliced video, texture-grid distortion; commercial AR masks for Google/Facebook (Spark AR); references Francis Thompson's 1957 film *N.Y., N.Y.* optical distortions ([2017](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/daily-sketches-in-2017-1b4234b0615d)).
- Bodies: OpenCV contour extraction with **resampling to constant point count + rotational alignment** frame-to-frame; contours lathed into 3D; horizontal-scanline cross sections; OpenPose skeletons on archival footage; ARKit body segmentation trails; Kinect Azure layering; dance collaborations (Daito Manabe/Elevenplay) ([2016](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/daily-sketches-2016-28586d8f008e), [2017](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/daily-sketches-in-2017-1b4234b0615d), [2019](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/daily-sketching-in-2019-c1c801045577), [2020](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/daily-sketching-in-2020-c2b4a59057e5)).

### 3.9 Slit-scan & AR
Slit-scan is a career-long motif (openFrameworks example ships with one): sample one row/column of the camera per frame and accumulate into time-space images. In 2017 ARKit sketches he used **camera pixels as paint deposited along the phone's path in 3D** — spatial slit-scanning, citing ART+COM's *The Invisible Shape of Things Past* (1995). Also AR audio placed in space ([Vimeo](https://vimeo.com/290238447)), photos re-placed at capture locations ([2017](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/daily-sketches-in-2017-1b4234b0615d)).

### 3.10 Curves, circles, tubes
Incremental-steering "endless lines" (turtle walk: step forward, turn by slowly varying angle, smooth) → drunken-walk organics; circles-through-3-points; **Dubins curves** for smooth constrained path interpolation between drawings; **stacked circles along a path** rendered with line-line intersections → tube/worm pseudo-3D forms (led to Adidas work); revolved half-circle arcs that "appear illuminated" without lighting; "circle push" grids where oversize circles bulge cells into hills, shader-lit ([2016](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/daily-sketches-2016-28586d8f008e), [2017](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/daily-sketches-in-2017-1b4234b0615d), [2021](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/daily-sketching-in-2021-fc92220aa3f4)).

### 3.11 Particles, dots, growth
Optimized particle-particle interaction for thousands-of-points blob fields (spatial hashing); dot fields; **physarum/slime-mold agent simulation** (2025 obsession: agents deposit into a trail map, sense ahead L/C/R, steer toward strongest trail, map diffuses+decays each frame — classic Jeff Jones algorithm, ideal as a WebGL compute/ping-pong shader) applied to typography and the body; Munari-style rule-based tree growth (branch, shorten, avoid collisions); fluid sim revisits; ribbons with varied shading (2022) ([2020](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/daily-sketching-in-2020-c2b4a59057e5), [2025](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/daily-sketching-in-2025-60189ffd6b60), [2022](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/daily-sketching-in-2022-4160eac26aa8)).

### 3.12 Working method for all of the above
Shader-first with **live reload**; "needling" — tiny parameter changes with big visual consequences; "turning the volume knob up to 11" then scaling back to find the essence; **contact-sheet workflow** — "make and capture, make and capture, and later find the best selections," like a nature photographer editing a shoot; combinatorial pairing of elements; deliberate 2D/3D ambiguity; anti-sterile bias — sketchy, wobbly, organic rendering over clean CG ([2018](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/daily-sketching-in-2018-f5de6d284aac), [2022](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/daily-sketching-in-2022-4160eac26aa8), [2024](https://zachlieberman.medium.com/daily-sketching-in-2024-77713b80f25d)).

## 6. Making Digital Work Look Non-Digital

Techniques Lieberman's circle (and DesLauriers/Hobbs especially) use to escape the "sterile CG" look Zach explicitly avoids:

- **Dithering** ([Ditherpunk — surma.dev](https://surma.dev/things/ditherpunk/), the canonical writeup; [Dither — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dither)):
  - **Bayer/ordered**: threshold each pixel against a tiled 2/4/8-size recursive matrix; crosshatch retro look; trivially shader-friendly (`step(bayer(uv), luminance)`); animation-stable (no crawling).
  - **Floyd–Steinberg** (1976): error diffusion 7/16 right, 3/16 down-left, 5/16 down, 1/16 down-right; organic but serial (CPU/canvas, not fragment shaders) and shimmers under animation.
  - **Blue noise**: threshold against a precomputed blue-noise texture ([free textures — momentsingraphics.de](https://momentsingraphics.de/BlueNoise.html)); organic like error diffusion but parallel and temporally stable — the best default for WebGL. Also use at low amplitude (±1/255) to kill gradient banding.
- **Grain**: film-grain overlay via animated hash noise, luminance-dependent (more in mids), ideally applied pre-quantization; `grain = hash(uv*res + frame)` mixed at 3–8%.
- **Paper texture**: multiply/overlay a scanned or procedural fibrous texture (low-freq blotches + high-freq fiber noise); slight per-channel edge darkening ("ink pooling"); displace strokes by a tiny paper-height field so lines wobble.
- **Risograph aesthetic** ([riso simulator — studio-ity.com](https://studio-ity.com/riso/)): limited spot-color inks (classic: fluorescent pink, blue, teal, sunflower yellow), **multiply blending** of separately halftoned/grained layers, deliberate **misregistration** (offset each ink layer 1–4px in a random direction), heavy grain in flat areas, uneven ink coverage (low-freq noise on opacity), paper white instead of #fff.
- **CMYK halftone misregistration**: separate to C/M/Y/K, halftone each at traditional screen angles (15°/75°/0°/45°), rotate/offset layers slightly, recombine multiplicatively — instant print feel; moiré between screens is a feature.
- **Plotter aesthetic**: everything is stroked lines, no fills — hatching/cross-hatching for tone; constant-ish line width; slight per-vertex jitter and low-pass "hand wobble"; occasional ink blobs at path starts; visible pen-lift gaps; limited pen palette. (See DesLauriers' plotter work and *Meridian*, [Right Click Save](https://www.rightclicksave.com/article/the-interview-matt-deslauriers-generative-art).)
- Zach's own versions of "non-digital": Epson P800 archival prints on Hot Press Bright paper ("prints reveal details missed on screens"), sketchy NPR rendering, noise-perturbed everything, and his 2024 mission statement — fighting the fact that "computational art is good at making images that feel like computational things."

**Practical WebGL ordering for the "non-digital" pass:** render clean → color grade → chromatic aberration (subtle, edges only) → grain → blue-noise dither/quantize → paper multiply + vignette.
